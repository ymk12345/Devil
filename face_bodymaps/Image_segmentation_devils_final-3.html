Methods Description:

We extracted tumor location and size of each diseased devil from available photos and field records known as devil fundamental sheets that were taken by field ecologists during their field work between 2003-2019. These fundamental sheets provide exact measurements of the length and width of each tumor as well as a rough sketch of its location on schematic representations of the devil face (front, left, right) and body (dorsal, ventral). Using this information and photos taken of the tumors of each devil, we drew the contours of the tumors onto blank versions of these devil facial and body diagrams with a drawing pad to refine the map of tumor locations in diseased devils.

We generated a custom python script to stack the tumor contour diagrams of the diseased devils to quantify the density of tumor occurrence across the devil face and body.

def ref_extract(filename):
    ref=cv2.imread(filename)
    img = cv2.cvtColor(ref, cv2.COLOR_BGR2GRAY)
    _,alpha = cv2.threshold(img,254,255,cv2.THRESH_BINARY_INV)
    b, g, r = cv2.split(ref)
    rgba = [b,g,r, alpha]
    dst = cv2.merge(rgba,4)
    return dst
    
    
def devil_plot(aggregate_file, devil_ref, vmin_man, vmax_man, pltcolor='viridis',colorcut=10, line = .3):
    cmap = cm.get_cmap(pltcolor, colorcut)    # 11 discrete colors
    plt.figure(figsize=(20, 8))
    plt.imshow(aggregate_file/255, cmap, vmin = vmin_man, vmax = vmax_man)
    plt.colorbar()
    plt.imshow(devil_ref)
    #ax=plt.gca()
    plt.grid(linestyle='-', linewidth=line, color='white')
